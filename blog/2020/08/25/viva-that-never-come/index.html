The Viva That Never Come

Posted on 2020-08-25   |   In diary   |  

The viva, the PhD viva, seems to be always around the corner. Unfortunately, it never come. I have experienced from full of expectation, to anxiety, and then to numbness. However helpless and reluctant I feel, I have no choice but accept the reality and wait.

Ever since the submission of the PhD thesis in March, I have been waiting for the viva, with both anticipation and fear. I though I might catch the July graduation ceremony. But now, after about six monthâ€™s wait, the viva date is still up in the air. I am very likely to miss the November ceremony, and will be delayed to graduate in the next year.

When I look back on those hard times in writing my thesis, even during my quarantine for the COVID-19 suspect, I never gave up writing and was still trying to finish the draft before the preset deadline. With both fear and hope, I finally finished it one month later. I was hoping to graduate in time. However, all my efforts are in vain with the unexpected natural disaster and human procrastination and mistakes. Maybe this is my doom.

However, I do have learned some lessons from this unpleasant experience, the situation of which might have been improved if I realized them and took action earlier. First, familiarize yourself with the process and figure out who is in charge and will impact the process. Second, never feel shy to urge the person in charge when you should or need. Third, emails are not always reliable, try face-to-face meeting when necessary, which is always more effective and efficient when there are obvious communication problems. Finally, make plans in advance, take action as soon as possible, and remember that the time should be counted in weeks not days to accommodate human needs and unexpected situations, especially when your superiors are involved.

Though there is high chance that I will miss the important deadline. But I will not give up the theoretical possibility. I will try my best to do my part and urge the people in charge. Hope I can make it. Wish me luck.
